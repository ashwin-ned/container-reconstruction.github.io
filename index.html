Abstract

Accurately estimating point clouds from a single image using monocular depth estimation is a critical challenge in computer vision with significant applications in robotics in areas such as autonomous navigation, augmented reality, and 3D reconstruction. This thesis presents a monocular vision architecture designed to estimate the free volume within shipping containers using RGB images captured from mobile devices, aiming to enhance cargo optimization and warehouse management for logistics. Leveraging recent advancements in deep learning, particularly in monocular depth estimation, this research proposes a cost-effective and scalable 3D reconstruction and volume estimation solution. The architecture integrates recent deep learning models fine-tuned with domain-specific data collected. The problem addressed is optimizing cargo loading, a critical aspect of logistics impacting sustainability and economic efficiency, which is traditionally reliant on manual measurements or specialized instruments. The proposed approach, grounded in computer vision techniques, seeks to overcome challenges such as depth perception ambiguity, occlusion, and lighting variations by accurately reconstructing watertight meshes of container interiors to measure free volume. The architecture can be broken down into two sub-problems, image-point cloud and point cloud-closed mesh, and encompasses three stages: 3D reconstruction, data collection and fine-tuning, and evaluation and benchmarking. The goal is to demonstrate the feasibility and advantages of monocular depth estimation for logistical applications, enhancing operational efficiency through precise volume estimation and optimized loading configurations. This work contributes to the field by exploring architectural improvements for monocular depth estimation pipelines by assessing their performance on real-world logistics tasks and providing a comprehensive evaluation framework, ultimately aiming to revolutionize efficiency in logistics.
